Insider Advice

Devon Wisdom

  • Book GWR train tickets 8+ weeks ahead — London Paddington to Exeter advance fares from ~£15 each way. A Family Railcard (£30/year) saves 33% adult + 60% child every journey.
  • The Transmoor Link bus (services 98/118) runs Exeter–Okehampton–Dartmoor — a Day Rover ticket covers all Dartmoor buses. Essential for car-free travel.
  • Lydford Gorge NT tickets must be booked online in advance for May–June. Free for National Trust members — an NT family membership (~£130/year) covers this, Dovedale, and many other spots on your bucket list.
  • Devon cream tea etiquette: cream first, then jam (Cornwall does it the other way — you're in Devon!). The Warren House Inn near Postbridge is the most atmospheric spot on the moor.
  • Wild Dartmoor ponies are protected — don't feed them. In May you'll very likely see mares with foals; keep a respectful distance and they'll often approach you naturally.
  • YHA Dartmoor is remote — stock up on food in Exeter before heading up. The hostel has a kitchen and sells basic supplies, but the nearest supermarket is ~6 miles away.
  • Dartmoor weather changes fast even in May — always pack waterproofs. The moor can be sunny at 9am and foggy by noon. Check the Met Office Dartmoor forecast each morning.
